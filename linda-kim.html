About Linda

Hi, I'm Linda!

I know that finding people who you can lean on when times get tough isn’t easy. Although part of my identity is as a clinician, I'm mainly just a person who has imperfections and wounds of my own that needed mending and was motivated by difficult situations to learn ways to promote healing in myself and others.

I have come a long way from where I once was and have so much gratitude for the life I get to live today. Whether it is supporting adolescents and adults, or supporting other clinicians in their work, my mission is to inspire hope, peace, and passion for living in others.

Core Values:

  • Reliability
  • Authenticity
  • Compassion

My Approach

During sessions, I prioritize giving you undivided attention, genuine care, and new skills. I love incorporating interventions that encourage you to challenge your thoughts, develop greater acceptance, practice somatic awareness, and face emotions that are often avoided.

I integrate a variety of therapy modalities and can also offer a faith-based Christian perspective for those who request it.

My Training

  • MSW, Fordham University (Clinical focus on individuals and families)
  • Trauma-Focused CBT (TF-CBT)
  • EMDR (EMDRIA-trained)
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)
  • ASIST Certification for suicide intervention

Highlighted Continuing Education: Trauma & Dissociation, CBT for Anxiety and Depression, CBT for Psychosis, OCD Treatment, Nutrition Psychiatry, Sleep & Mental Health

My Background

Linda has worked with children through adults experiencing PTSD, anxiety, phobias, and mood disorders. Her experience includes family trauma therapy at Tree House CAC, outpatient therapy at Bethesda Counseling Services, and crisis counseling at CCSI.

Outside of Therapy

If I'm not in session, I’m probably critiquing food, thrifting, enjoying the outdoors, or relaxing with my family.
